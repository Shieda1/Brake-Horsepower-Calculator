What is the brake horsepwer?
Brake horsepower is the horsepower of an engine measured by the degree of resistance offered by a brake that represents the useful power that the machine can develop.
How to calculate brake horsepwer?
To calculate brake horsepwer you should use the following formula:
BHP = (Q * H * SG) / (3960 * eff)
where:
BHP = brake horsepower
H = pump head (ft)
Q = flow (gpm) gallons per minute
SG = specific gravity of the fluid being pumped
eff = pump's efficiency
Why use brake horsepwer?
The brake horsepwer is used to ensure that the output of a motor is strong enough to drive both the motor and any auxiliary components and it is also used to determine the power that must be produced to allow the motor to funciton at peak efficiency with the core funcitons.
